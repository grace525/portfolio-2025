La Sportiva Logo Animation

An animation exercise focused on adding movement to an existing logo.


Animation Principles

This animation utilizes a few of Disney’s 12 Principles Of Animation:

1. Squash and Stretch
A key principle in bringing animation to life, squash and stretch create the illusion of weight and elasticity. In this example, the mountain appears to "bounce" out of the ground, stretching as gravity pulls it upward before squashing slightly as it settles into place.


2. Anticipation
What signals tell us something is just about to happen? Anticipation holds the audience’s attention and builds curiosity. In this example, the delay in the "V" transforming into the "A" creates a moment of suspense, drawing focus to the transition.


3. Follow Through
Follow-through is evident when the mountain bounces out of the ground, lands, and then continues with a smaller secondary bounce. This subtle motion reinforces the effect of gravity, making the animation feel more organic.


4. Exaggeration
Animation allows artists to push the boundaries of reality. By exaggerating movements, the motion feels more dynamic and engaging rather than strictly realistic. In this animation, the mountain's dramatic bounce and the playful unfolding of the "V" into an "A" add energy and character to the sequence through exaggeration.
