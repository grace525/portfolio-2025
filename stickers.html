Website Redesign

Re-imagining the Boulder Sports Recycler mobile website


Overview

Software: Figma
Timeline: 2 weeks

The Boulder Sports Recycler mobile website has a nostalgic aesthetic but is difficult to navigate. The redesign improves functionality while maintaining the brand’s character through a retro-inspired color palette and relevant imagery. Content is now divided across multiple pages to replace the original single-scrolling page. Anchor links have been added to allow quick navigation within pages. New sections provide first-time customers with clear information about the business’s offerings, location, and mission.

Problem

  1. Hard-to-read font
  2. Overwhelming color scheme
  3. Difficult to find specific information
  4. Missing key details (e.g., business mission and services)

Solution

  1. Used readable fonts with clear text hierarchy
  2. Applied a curated, cohesive color scheme
  3. Organized content across multiple pages
  4. Added anchor links for faster navigation



Site Flow

Organize content and divide across multliple pages.

Branding Guidelines

Maintain vintage outdoor design elements but improve readablilty

Reflection

Re-designing the Boulder Sports Recycler website presented some unique challenges. My goal was to streamline the user experience while still capturing the brand's adventurous spirit. I aimed to tame the original site’s overwhelming information-dump and focus on creating a more functional, user-friendly layout. By making the content clearer and more accessible, I hoped to improve overall navigation without sacrificing the brand’s personality.

One of the biggest lessons I learned during the process was how crucial it is to consider how designs translate across devices. While the text size looked great on my laptop screen, it felt much larger than intended when tested on a smaller screen. I also found it valuable to reference the design on a mobile device to better understand how images should be sized, how large margins should be, etc. I discovered that larger margins feel more natural and align better with the fast scrolling behavior on smartphones, making the content easier to read while quickly skimming the site. When margins are too small, the content feels cramped, and the site becomes tedious to scroll through.

Another key takeaway was the importance of consistency across all pages. While it’s easy to get lost in the details when organizing large amounts of text, keeping margins, spacing, fonts, and colors consistent is essential. When something feels “off” about a layout, it’s often because these basic but crucial elements have been overlooked. Ensuring alignment consistency throughout the design gives the final product a polished, cohesive look.

In the end, the redesign is more navigable, visually appealing, and true to the brand’s core values. That said, it may ruffle the feathers of loyal fans who appreciate the original site’s eccentric, retro, handmade vibe. But for those who don’t want to spend their evening sifting through the original site’s chaotic information, this redesign is for you.
